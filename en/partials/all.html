Wines

Winemaking: Harvest takes place throughout the month of September. Grapes are hand-picked in 20 kg crates and vinified separately. Each wine undergoes fermentation in stainless steel tanks with precise temperature control, then matures for varying periods in French oak barrels — both new and second-use — to achieve balance and complexity.

Vintage Characteristics in the Douro Superior:

  • 2018 – A climate between 2015 and 2016, offering a balance between concentration and elegance.
  • 2017 – Even hotter than 2015; our wines from this vintage are still resting.
  • 2016 – Reminiscent of 2014, with little rainfall. Wines show fresher red fruit, less concentration and volume on the palate, yet marked by elegance and freshness.
  • 2015 - A hotter year during the pre-harvest and harvest period. Grapes were riper and darker, producing wines with greater concentration.
  • 2014 – Mild temperatures resulted in fresher, more elegant wines.
