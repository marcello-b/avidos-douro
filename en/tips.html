Wine Tips

Make the Most of Every Bottle

Expert Advice

Learn how to choose, serve, and pair wines with our specialists’ guidance. Discover how to extract the very best from each bottle and impress your guests.

Serving Temperature

Full-bodied reds: 16–18°C

Light reds: 14–16°C

Full-bodied whites: 10–12°C

Light whites: 8–10°C

Sparkling wines: 6–8°C

Dessert wines: 6–10°C

The ideal temperature enhances the wine’s aromas and flavors. When in doubt, serve slightly cooler—wine naturally warms in the glass.

Opening and Aeration

Young wines: Open 30 minutes before serving

Aged wines: Open shortly before serving

Use a decanter: For wines with sediment or very young wines

Allowing wine to breathe helps develop its aromas and soften the tannins. However, very old wines may lose their character if exposed to air for too long.

The Right Glass

Full-bodied reds: Large, rounded glasses

White wines: Medium tulip-shaped glasses

Sparkling wines: Flutes or tulip-shaped glasses

Port wines: Small tulip-shaped glasses

The correct glass shape helps concentrate the aromas and guide the wine to the right areas of the palate, enhancing the tasting experience.

Pairing

Red meats: Full-bodied reds

White meats: Light reds or full-bodied whites

Fish and seafood: White wines or rosés

Aged cheeses: Full-bodied reds or Port wine

Desserts: Sweet wines or sparkling wines

The basic rule is to pair light wines with lighter dishes and robust wines with more intense flavors. But don’t be afraid to experiment!

Storage

Temperature: Constant between 12–16°C

Position: Bottles laid horizontally (for cork closure)

Light: Away from direct light

Humidity: Around 70%

Vibration: Avoid places with vibration

Proper storage is essential for wine quality. If you don’t have a cellar, choose a cool, dark place away from heat sources.

How To Taste

1. Visual: Observe the color and clarity

2. Nose: Smell the aromas before swirling

3. Swirl: Gently swirl the glass to release aromas

4. Second nose: Identify the different aromas

5. Taste: Take a small sip and let it spread across your mouth

6. Finish: Note the persistence of flavors

Wine tasting is an art that develops with practice. Don’t be intimidated. Trust your senses and enjoy the experience!

General Tips

  • Always have fresh water available to cleanse the palate between tastings
  • Avoid strong perfumes when tasting wine
  • Do not fill the glass more than one-third – this allows aromas to concentrate
  • Open sparkling wines carefully, at a 45-degree angle
  • Store leftover wine with proper stoppers and consume within 2–3 days
  • Write down your impressions – it helps develop your palate
  • There are no absolute rules – the best wine is the one you enjoy!
