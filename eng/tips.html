Opening and Breathing

Young wines: Open 30 minutes before serving

Aged wines: Open shortly before serving

Use a decanter: For wines with sediment or very young wines

Letting the wine breathe helps develop its aromas and soften the tannins. However, very old wines can lose their characteristics if exposed to air for too long.

Correct Glass

Full-bodied red wines: Large, rounded glasses

White wines: Medium, tulip-shaped glasses

Sparkling wines: Flutes or tulip glasses

Port wines: Small, tulip-shaped glasses

The correct glass shape helps concentrate aromas and directs the wine to the right areas of the palate, enhancing the tasting experience.

How to Taste

1. Visual: Observe color and clarity

2. Olfactory: Smell the aromas before swirling

3. Rotation: Swirl the glass to release aromas

4. Second smell: Identify the different aromas

5. Taste: Take a small sip and let it spread in mouth

6. Finish: Notice the aftertaste
